The Nikko Symposium (1955)

In September 1955, at the International Symposium on Algebraic Number Theory held in Nikko, Japan, Yutaka Taniyama posed several profound problems that eventually led to the Modularity Theorem.

Statement of the Problems

Problem 1

Let $k$ be a finite algebraic number field containing a primitive $n$-th root of unity. Assuming $\mathfrak{p} \nmid n$, prove the product formula $\prod_{\mathfrak{p}} \left( \frac{a,b}{\mathfrak{p}} \right) = 1$ directly for the specific cases where $a=1$ or $b \equiv 1 \pmod n$, without relying on the results of class field theory. (Ichiro Satake)

Problem 2

Let $\mathfrak{c}$ be an ideal class of an algebraic number field $k$. What kind of abelian extension field of $k$ will principalize $\mathfrak{c}$? Find a sufficient condition for this to occur. (Fumiyuki Terada)

Problem 3

Let $k$ be a finite algebraic number field, $A$ be the maximal abelian extension over $k$, and $G_k$ be the Galois group of $A/k$. For two fields $k_1$ and $k_2$, what is the (necessary or sufficient) condition for $G_{k_1} \cong G_{k_2}$ to hold either algebraically or topologically? (Tomio Kubota)

Problem 4

Does there exist a finite algebraic number field that possesses an infinite unramified extension field? If such a field exists, provide a concrete example. (Tomio Kubota)

Problem 5

Let $k$ be a $\mathfrak{p}$-adic number field, and for a prime $p$, let $\Omega$ be the maximal $p$-extension field over $k$. Determine the Galois group $G = G(\Omega/k)$ explicitly. What is the structure of the Galois group $G(\Omega/\mathbb{Q}_p)$ when $\Omega$ is the algebraic closure of $\mathbb{Q}_p$? (Y. Kawada, building on partial results by Šafarevič and Skopin)

Problem 6

Construct class field theory over $k$ when $k$ is an infinite algebraic extension field of the rational number field $\mathbb{Q}$ (or a $\mathfrak{p}$-adic extension field $\mathbb{Q}_p$). The goal is to build a unified theory that encompasses both finite extensions and Kummer theories of infinite extensions. (Kenkichi Iwasawa, Y. Kawada)

Problem 7

Let $k$ be a function field of one variable over a finite constant field $\text{GF}(p)$. Construct class field theory over $k$ using algebraic geometry. Specifically: (i) Construct a unified method that applies to both extensions with degrees coprime to $p$ and those involving powers of $p$ (which currently require Witt vectors). This might require some theory of fibre spaces. (ii) Extend the theory to include inseparable extensions. (iii) Consider the formulation for higher-dimensional algebraic varieties. (André Weil)

Problem 8

Let $K$ be the field of meromorphic functions on a non-singular algebraic variety embedded in a projective space. Construct the theory of class formations for unramified extensions over $K$. (Y. Kawada)

Problem 9

Let $k$ be a $\mathfrak{p}$-adic number field, $K$ an abelian extension over $k$, and $D$ a normal division algebra over $k$ with rank $[K:k]^2$ such that $D \supset K$. For odd $[K:k]$, there is an isomorphism $D^*/K^* \cdot D^{*\prime} \cong G(K/k)$, which can be viewed as an isomorphism theorem in local class field theory. Is it possible to consider a similar isomorphism for non-abelian extensions $K/k$? (Tadao Tannaka)

Problem 10

Let the Galois group $G = G(K/k)$ of a relative abelian field $K/k$ have two generators, with $K_1$ and $K_2$ being intermediate fields corresponding to the direct product components of $G$. If we assume $H^3(G, K^*) = 1$, we obtain $N_{K_1/k}(K_1^*) \cap N_{K_2/k}(K_2^*) = N_{K/k}(K^*)$. This equation perfectly mirrors the statement of local class field theory, indicating a deep relationship between the 3rd cohomology group and local class field theory. Investigate these profound connections further. (Tadao Tannaka)

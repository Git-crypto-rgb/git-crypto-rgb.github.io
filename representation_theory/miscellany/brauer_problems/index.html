Introduction to the 46 Problems

In 1963, Richard Brauer published a list of 46 open problems concerning the representation theory of finite groups, particularly focusing on modular representations and block theory.

Problem 1: The k(B) Conjecture

Let $B$ be a $p$-block of a finite group $G$ with defect group $D$. Brauer conjectured that the number of irreducible ordinary characters in $B$, denoted $k(B)$, satisfies $k(B) \le |D|$.

Application of homological algebra

Extension problems

A fundamental question in algebra is how to construct an object from little building blocks. This story begins with an abelian category $\mathscr{A}$. Given two objects $A$ and $C$ as objects of $\mathscr{A}$, find all middle objects $E$ that fits into the exact sequence $$0 \longrightarrow A \longrightarrow E \longrightarrow C\longrightarrow 0$$ This is known as classification problem.

We begin with the linear case, $R$-modules $A$ and $C$. The notes provide a structural classification via $\mathrm{Ext}$ functor, which suggests the set of equivalence classes of the short exact sequences are isomorphic to the group $\mathrm{Ext}^1(C,A)$.

Unsolved problems

Jacobian conjecture

Statement: This problem is related to $\mathrm{Aut}(\mathbb{A}^2)$. Let $k$ be a field of characteristic $0$. Then, a map defined by $$x'= f(x,y), \quad y'= g(x,y)$$ with $f,g\in k[x,y]$ is an element of $\mathrm{Aut}(\mahtbb{A}^2(k))$ $\LR$ the Jacobian determinant $\frac{\partial (f,g)}{\partial (x,y)}$ is a non-zero constant.
A similar version is for $\mathrm{Aut}(\mathbb{A}^n(k))$.

Progress: this conjecture is proved for $f$ and $g$ with lower degrees ($\deg f, \deg g$ is less than 100).
